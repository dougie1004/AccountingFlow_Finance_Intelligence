# 💡 Key Lessons & Strategic Pivot (Simulation Conclusion)
**Date:** 2026-02-04
**Verified By:** Simulation Data (2026-2028)

## 1. The "SaaS Myth" Busted
*   **Hypothesis:** "If we make a good SaaS product, users will subscribe, and we will break even by 2027."
*   **Simulation Result:** **False.**
    *   Low ARPU (39,900 KRW) cannot cover the high fixed costs of an B2B operation (High-availability Server, Skilled Engineers, Rent).
    *   The "Death Valley" (Cash crunch) in 2027 is unavoidable with pure SaaS metrics.

## 2. The "SI Reality" Confirmed
*   **Hypothesis:** "We need to do Customization/SI (System Integration) to survive and build value."
*   **Simulation Result:** **True.**
    *   **Cash Flow:** SI contracts (e.g., 9.5M/mo) provide the necessary upfront cash to fund R&D without diluting equity cheaply.
    *   **Profitability:** Turning a 50M deficit into a 100M profit was *only* possible by adding the Service/Construction revenue layer.
    *   **Market Fit:** B2B Accounting requires specific logic per company. Generic SaaS churns; Custom Logic sticks.

## 3. What This Means for "AccountingFlow" (The Product)
Since our business model is **"SI-First, SaaS-Later"**, our internal accounting tool must excel at:
1.  **Project Accounting:** Tracking P&L *per client project*, not just company-wide.
2.  **Progress Billing (기성고):** Handling "Down payment -> Progress -> Final" billing cycles, which differ from simple monthly subscriptions.
3.  **Labor Allocation:** Tracking how much engineering time went into "Customer A's SI" (COGS) vs "Core SaaS" (R&D).

> **CEO's Insight:**
> *"SaaS is difficult. We must do SI. That's the way it is supposed to be."*
