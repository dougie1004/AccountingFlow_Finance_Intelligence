## 2. Why "Pure SaaS" Failed in the Simulation?
(Without the "Enterprise SI" revenue and "Government Grant" accounting defenses)

### A. The "ARPU vs. Burn" Mismatch
*   **Average Revenue Per User (ARPU):** ~39,900 KRW (Standard Plan focus)
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Implicitly high due to the marketing budget (40M) relative to slow initial growth.
*   **Fixed Cost Heavy:** Even with a "Lean" team, the base fixed costs (Labor 160M + Rent 30M + AWS High Availability) created a burn rate that required **~800+ active paying users just to break even.**
*   **Result:** The "J-Curve" was too shallow. By the time user count hit 800 (mid-2028), the cumulative deficits from 2026-2027 had already eroded capital efficiency.

### B. The "2027 Death Valley"
*   **Scenario:** 2027 was the most critical year. Operating expenses jumped (Team expansion, Office move) but subscriber revenue lagged by 6-9 months (The "SaaS Ramp-up Lag").
*   **Outcome:** Without the **9.5M/month SI Revenue** injection we added, 2027 would have resulted in a **Net Loss of ~50M KRW**, likely forcing a down-round investment or bankruptcy.

## 3. The "Hybrid Survival" Strategy (Pivot)
The simulation proved that **Subscription Revenue alone is too slow** to cover the initial fixed costs. To make this business viable (as shown in the final "Green" numbers), the specific adjustments we made are **Mandatory, not Optional**:

1.  **Revenue Mix Pivot (Critical):**
    *   **MUST DO:** You cannot rely 100% on SaaS. You **must** run a parallel "Enterprise/SI" track generating at least **100M KRW/year** (approx 8~9M/month) in 2027-2028.
    *   *Simulation Evidence:* This was the single factor that turned 2027 from Red to Black.

2.  **Accounting Defense:**
    *   **MUST DO:** Government grants (2026) must be treated as "Expense Offsets" (Net accounting), not just Liabilities, to keep the P/L clean for Series A investors.

3.  **Cost Discipline:**
    *   **MUST DO:** AWS/Infrastructure costs must be capped at **5M/month** in 2028. The original 10M/month forecast would have wiped out 60% of the operating profit.
